Cristiano Ronaldo

The world's greatest footballer

Here's a timeline of Cristiano Ronaldo's career

  • 2001 - 2003: At the age of 16, Cristiano Ronaldo made a nervous debut for Sporting CP, making 25 appearances and scoring 3 goals between 2001 and 2003.
  • 2003: After impressing with his skills in a friendly match against Manchester United, Cristiano Ronaldo joined the club for £12.24 million, as recommended by players and Sir Alex Ferguson himself.
  • 2008: Ronaldo's remarkable season at Manchester United saw him score 42 goals, earning him the Golden Boot award for Europe's top goalscorer, and winning the Ballon d'Or, making him the first United player since George Best in 1968 to achieve this feat. His standout performance in the Champions League final against Chelsea further solidified his status as one of the best players in the world.
  • 2009: Ronaldo, after feeling he had accomplished everything at Manchester United, made a high-profile transfer to Real Madrid for a record-breaking fee of £80 million, where he was welcomed by 80,000 fans at the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium, marking the beginning of his journey with the Spanish club.
  • 2011: Despite a disappointing World Cup in 2010, Ronaldo maintained his exceptional form at Real Madrid, earning the La Liga Golden Shoe award for scoring the most goals in the season. He demonstrated his charitable side by donating the Golden Boots to support children in Gaza, Palestine.
